CONSULT 8 — Phase Margin Analysis & Compensation Strategy
=========================================================
Date: 2026-04-03

CURRENT MEASURED PERFORMANCE (from Bode plot)
---------------------------------------------
- DC gain:        70.8 dB
- Estimated UGB:  ~142 kHz (extrapolated)
- Phase at 10kHz: ~-92 degrees
- Estimated PM:   ~50 degrees

PROBLEM STATEMENT
-----------------
For no ringing in transient response, PM should be >65 degrees.
  - Critically damped response: PM = 76 degrees
  - Acceptable for most applications: PM >= 65 degrees
  - Current PM: ~50 degrees — INSUFFICIENT

Need to add ~15-25 degrees of phase margin.

CLOSED-LOOP Q ANALYSIS
-----------------------
With PM = 50 degrees at UGB:
  Q = 1 / (2 * sin(PM/2))
  Q = 1 / (2 * sin(25°))
  Q = 1 / (2 * 0.4226)
  Q = 1.183

Q > 1 produces peaking and oscillatory step response:
  - Peaking = ~1.4 dB in closed-loop frequency response
  - PSRR plot confirms: resonant peak at ~30 kHz
  - This frequency is where loop gain crosses 0 dB with inadequate PM

Target: PM > 60 degrees for Q < 1 (no peaking)
Ideal:  PM >= 65 degrees for well-damped response

COMPENSATION OPTIONS
--------------------

Option 1: Reduce UGB by increasing Cc (lower bandwidth)
  - Increases Cc to push the dominant pole lower
  - Moves UGB to a frequency where phase erosion is less
  - Trade-off: reduced bandwidth, slower transient response
  - May degrade load transient recovery time

Option 2: Add a zero below UGB for phase boost
  - Place a compensation zero at f_z < UGB to add positive phase
  - Typically via series R-C in the compensation network (Rc-Cc)
  - If Rc already exists, increase Rc to lower the zero frequency
  - Zero frequency: f_z = 1 / (2*pi*Rc*Cc)
  - Target: place zero at ~0.3-0.5x UGB for maximum phase boost at UGB
  - For UGB ~142 kHz, place zero at ~50-70 kHz

Option 3: Feedforward capacitor across R_TOP (feedback resistor)
  - Adds a zero-pole pair in the feedback network
  - Zero: f_z = 1 / (2*pi*R_TOP*C_ff)
  - Provides phase lead near UGB
  - Also improves high-frequency PSRR
  - Trade-off: may increase noise gain at high frequencies

RECOMMENDED APPROACH
--------------------
Combine Options 2 and 3:

1. Adjust Rc to place compensation zero at ~50-70 kHz
   - This provides ~15-20 degrees of phase boost at UGB
   - Directly addresses the PSRR resonant peak at 30 kHz

2. Add small feedforward cap C_ff across R_TOP
   - Provides additional 5-10 degrees of phase lead
   - Improves PSRR at high frequencies
   - Size C_ff so the zero is near UGB: C_ff = 1/(2*pi*R_TOP*f_UGB)

3. Verify PM > 65 degrees after compensation changes

PSRR RESONANT PEAK CORRELATION
-------------------------------
The PSRR resonant peak at ~30 kHz directly correlates with the
loop gain crossover and inadequate phase margin:
  - At frequencies near UGB where loop gain is low and PM is poor,
    the closed-loop rejection degrades
  - Improving PM will flatten the PSRR peak
  - Target: PSRR peak should be < 0 dB (no peaking above DC level)

VERIFICATION PLAN
-----------------
After compensation changes:
  1. Re-run AC Bode analysis — confirm PM > 65 degrees
  2. Re-run PSRR analysis — confirm resonant peak is eliminated
  3. Run load transient — confirm no ringing on step response
  4. Check across PVT corners — PM should be > 55 degrees worst-case
