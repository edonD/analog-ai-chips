ISSUE: ngspice simulations timing out repeatedly (3 attempts, 47+ minutes wasted)

ROOT CAUSE LIKELY:
1. XMcs_n has m=50 multiplier (w=20µ l=8µ × 50) — this creates 50 parallel instances
   that ngspice must solve individually. Very expensive.
2. Transient sim runs 0→500ms — that's very long for initial debugging.
3. The 1µF external cap + feedback loop may cause convergence oscillation.

RECOMMENDED FIXES:
1. Replace m=50 with single wider device: w=1000e-6 l=8e-6 (same W/L ratio, 1 device)
   Or better: w=100e-6 l=0.8e-6 m=1 (same gm, faster sim)
2. Shorten transient sim to 100µs for initial debugging (enough to see BVDD ramp + settling)
3. Add .options reltol=0.01 abstol=1e-10 vntol=1e-4 for faster convergence
4. Add .options ITL1=300 ITL2=200 for more DC iterations
5. Consider .tran 100n 100u UIC to skip DC operating point and use initial conditions

ALTERNATIVE APPROACH:
- Do a .DC sweep first (much faster) to verify the EA output vs PVDD relationship
- Only run transient once DC operating point is confirmed correct
