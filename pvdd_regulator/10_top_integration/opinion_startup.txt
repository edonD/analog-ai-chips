========================================================================
QUESTION 1: Is this the EA output fighting the BVDD pullup (Ren_ea 100Ω)?
========================================================================

NO. Ren_ea (100Ω) is NOT the cause.

Ren_ea connects BVDD to ea_en (the error amp enable pin). Inside the EA:
  - XMpu (PFET, gate=en) pulls vout_gate to BVDD when en is LOW (disabled)
  - Since ea_en is held at BVDD by Ren_ea, Vsg of XMpu = 0 → XMpu is OFF

The 100Ω resistor only carries whatever leakage current the ea_en node
draws (sub-µA gate leakage). It does NOT interact with the EA output
signal path. The ea_en is purely an enable/disable control, and at HIGH
it simply keeps the EA active. There is no mechanism by which Ren_ea
creates a pullup on the gate node.

However, there IS a pullup fighting the EA — see root cause below.

========================================================================
QUESTION 2: Is the 1k Rgate creating problems?
========================================================================

YES — Rgate (1kΩ) is a critical aggravating factor, though not root cause.

Rgate sits between ea_out and gate (in Block 09 startup circuit, line 19).
The current limiter clamp PFETs connect DIRECTLY to the gate node
(Block 04, lines 58-61: 4× PFET W=50µm L=0.5µm = 200µm total width).

This creates a severe asymmetry:
  - EA drives gate through 1kΩ series resistance → limited slew current
  - Clamp PFETs pull gate to BVDD with near-zero output impedance

When the current limiter fires, the clamp PFETs easily overpower the EA
output through the 1kΩ isolation resistor. The EA can only deliver:

    I_gate = (ea_out − gate) / Rgate = (0V − 7V) / 1kΩ = −7 mA max

The 4× clamp PFETs (200µm total, L=0.5µm) in saturation can source
tens of mA. The clamp always wins the tug-of-war.

Rgate was intended for "isolation and limiting gate charge/discharge rate"
(per Block 09 comments). In practice, it isolates the EA from its own
output — the EA cannot maintain gate control when ANY other driver
contends at the gate node.

========================================================================
QUESTION 3: What is the oscillation frequency and corresponding resonance?
========================================================================

OSCILLATION FREQUENCY: ~12–15 kHz (estimated from plot, ~70-100 cycles
in 0–7ms window)

This is NOT a linear resonance. It is a NONLINEAR LIMIT CYCLE caused by
the current limiter and error amplifier fighting over the gate node.
The frequency is set by the total loop propagation delay of the
current-limiter feedback path, not by any LC/RC resonance.

The limit-cycle loop delay consists of:
  1. EA drives gate LOW through Rgate (1kΩ × Cgg_pass ≈ 1kΩ × ~5pF ≈ 5ns)
     — fast, but EA output slew-limited by Stage 2 bias current (~23µA)
  2. Pass device turns on, current into Cout begins ramping
     — Cout=1µF, so I=C×dV/dt; at ~100mA inrush, dV/dt = 100V/s
  3. Current limiter sense mirror (W=1µm) + cascode settles
     — mirror NMOS (W=100µm L=8µm) has large Cgs, slow settling
  4. Comparator inverter (XMinv) switches
  5. Clamp PFETs activate, yank gate to BVDD (fast, ~ns)
  6. Pass device turns off, current drops
  7. Sense mirror current decays, mirror settles to new state
  8. Comparator releases, clamp PFETs turn off
  9. EA begins pulling gate back down through Rgate (slow)
  10. Repeat from step 1

The dominant delay contributions are:
  - Steps 3,7: Current mirror settling (large L=8µm NMOS, ~5-10µs)
  - Steps 1,9: EA recovery through Rgate (EA slew into 1kΩ load)
  - Steps 2,6: Cout charging/discharging (1µF is slow)

Total half-period ≈ 30-40µs → f ≈ 12-17 kHz. Consistent with observation.

The small-signal UGB at regulation is ~30 kHz and phase margin is ~67°.
The loop is perfectly stable in small-signal terms. This oscillation is
a large-signal startup transient phenomenon, invisible in AC analysis.

========================================================================
ROOT CAUSE DIAGNOSIS
========================================================================

The root cause is a STARTUP INRUSH LIMIT CYCLE between the error
amplifier and the current limiter. The mechanism:

1. During startup, PVDD ≈ 0V. The feedback voltage vfb ≈ 0V.
   The soft-start reference vref_ss is ramping (even a few mV > 0).

2. The EA has high DC gain (~20dB open-loop, but at DC with integrating
   behavior the gain is much higher). Any positive (vref_ss − vfb)
   saturates the EA output LOW (near ground).

3. With ea_out ≈ 0V and Rgate = 1kΩ, the gate is pulled to ~0V + IR drop.
   Vsg_pass = BVDD − gate ≈ 7V → pass device fully ON in triode.

4. The 1mm-wide pass PMOS (10× W=50µm L=0.5µm m=2) in triode with
   Vds = BVDD − PVDD delivers HUNDREDS of milliamps into the 1µF Cout.
   This massively exceeds the 50mA current limit threshold.

5. The current limiter detects overcurrent:
   - Sense PMOS mirrors Ipass/1000 → sense current >> 50µA threshold
   - Comparator trips → det_n goes LOW
   - 4× clamp PFETs (200µm) yank gate to BVDD

6. Gate at BVDD → Vsg = 0 → pass device OFF → current drops to zero.

7. Current limiter releases (det_n goes HIGH, clamp OFF).

8. EA immediately drives gate LOW again (vfb still << vref_ss).

9. Cycle repeats at ~12-15 kHz.

The key insight: the soft-start reference (Rss × Css, tau=2.2ms) does
NOT provide soft-start of the CURRENT. It soft-starts the voltage
TARGET. But the EA has enough gain that even the tiniest positive error
(vref_ss − vfb) saturates the output, commanding full pass-device
current. The actual output voltage rise rate is limited by the current
limiter, not by the soft-start reference — and the current limiter does
this through violent oscillation rather than clean limiting.

The oscillation decays and stops at ~7ms because:
  - As PVDD rises toward 5V, vfb approaches vref_ss
  - The EA error signal shrinks, EA comes out of saturation
  - The EA no longer drives gate to the rail
  - The loop transitions from bang-bang limit cycle to linear regulation
  - Current demand drops below 50mA → current limiter disengages

========================================================================
QUESTION 4: How to fix the gate oscillation
========================================================================

FIX OPTION A — Gate Snubber Capacitor (Simplest, Lowest Risk)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Add a capacitor directly on the gate node:

    Cgate_snub gate gnd 100p    (or gate bvdd 100p)

Effect: Slows the gate slew rate, converting the bang-bang oscillation
into a smooth ramp. With 100pF and the clamp PFETs sourcing ~10mA:
    dV/dt = I/C = 10mA/100pF = 100V/µs → still fast for startup
    But combined with Rgate=1kΩ: τ = 1kΩ × 100pF = 100ns

This may not be enough. A larger value (1-10nF) would smooth more
aggressively but would also slow load transient response. Trade-off:
  100pF: Minimal smoothing, may not kill oscillation
  1nF:   τ = 1µs through Rgate, should damp limit cycle
  10nF:  τ = 10µs, definitely kills oscillation but load transient
         response degrades (gate cannot slew faster than ~1V/10µs)

Recommended: 1nF as starting point. Verify load transient still meets
spec (10mA step, ΔVout < 100mV).


FIX OPTION B — Disable Current Limiter During Startup (Clean Fix)
------------------------------------------------------------------
The mode control already outputs ilim_en (from COMP4, TH4=5.6V BVDD).
Currently unused — per design.cir line 142:
  "NOTE: ilim_en is output by mode control but not connected."

Add an enable input to the current limiter. Gate the clamp PFETs with
ilim_en so they only activate after BVDD > 5.6V and PVDD is up:

In current_limiter.cir, gate the clamp PFETs:
  - Add ilim_en port
  - AND det_n with ilim_en_b (inverted ilim_en)
  - Or simply: gate the clamp PFET gates with a series NFET controlled
    by ilim_en_b

Risk: During the startup window (0 to ~5ms), there is NO overcurrent
protection. If a short circuit occurs during startup, the pass device
could be damaged. This is the standard trade-off for startup sequencing.

Mitigation: Keep a higher (coarse) current limit always active using a
simple resistor-sensed clamp (e.g., 200mA), and only enable the
precision 50mA limiter via ilim_en.


FIX OPTION C — Reduce Rgate (Moderate Improvement)
----------------------------------------------------
Replace Rgate = 1kΩ with 100Ω or 50Ω.

This lets the EA maintain gate control against the clamp PFETs.
With Rgate=100Ω, EA can deliver:
    I = 7V / 100Ω = 70mA into the gate node

This is comparable to what the clamp PFETs deliver, so the EA and clamp
reach a steady-state compromise rather than oscillating. The gate would
settle to an intermediate voltage where the pass device current equals
exactly the current limit threshold.

Risk: Lower Rgate means the gate node is more tightly coupled to ea_out.
Any noise or ringing on ea_out propagates to the gate. The original
purpose of Rgate was isolation — removing it couples the EA more tightly
to the large Cgs of the pass device, potentially affecting EA stability
(adding a large capacitive load to Stage 2).

EA Stage 2 bias current is ~23µA. Driving Cgs_pass (~5pF) through 100Ω:
    f_pole = 1/(2π × 100 × 5p) = 318 MHz — no AC concern at all.

But the clamp PFETs connecting directly to gate still create a
disturbance. This fix reduces oscillation amplitude but may not
eliminate it entirely.


FIX OPTION D — Soft-Start Current Ramp (Most Robust, Most Complex)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Replace the current limit reference with a ramping reference:

Instead of a fixed 1µA ibias_ilim, use a filtered version:
    Iibias_ilim 0 ibias_ilim_raw 1u
    Rss_ilim ibias_ilim_raw ibias_ilim 100k    (or xhigh_po)
    Css_ilim ibias_ilim gnd 10n

This ramps ibias_ilim from 0 to 1µA with tau = 100k × 10n = 1ms.
The current limit threshold ramps from 0mA to 50mA over ~5ms.

Effect: During early startup, the current limit is very low (near 0),
so the pass device can only trickle current into Cout. PVDD rises
slowly and smoothly. As ibias_ilim ramps up, the current limit
threshold increases, and by the time PVDD nears regulation, the full
50mA limit is available.

This is the most robust approach because:
  - Current is always limited (no protection gap like Option B)
  - No oscillation (limit tracks available current smoothly)
  - PVDD rise is monotonic and predictable

Downside: Adds components and increases startup time. With tau=1ms,
startup current limit reaches 50mA at ~5ms. PVDD charging at an average
of ~25mA into 1µF: ΔV = I×t/C = 25mA×5ms/1µF = 125V — obviously the
cap charges to 5V well within this time.


FIX OPTION E — Combined: Rgate Reduction + Gate Capacitor (Recommended)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Reduce Rgate from 1kΩ to 200Ω AND add Cgate_snub = 500pF on the gate:

    Rgate ea_out gate 200        (was 1k)
    Cgate_snub gate bvdd 500p    (new)

Rationale:
  - 200Ω Rgate: EA can deliver 35mA to contest the clamp, preventing
    full bang-bang. Still provides some isolation.
  - 500pF on gate: τ_gate = 200Ω × 500pF = 100ns through Rgate.
    Clamp sees 500pF → slew limited to ~10mA/500pF = 20V/µs.
    Gate transitions become ~100ns-scale, well within any limit cycle
    period, effectively converting oscillation to a DC operating point.

AC impact check at regulation:
  - Gate pole with 200Ω + 500pF: f = 1/(2π×200×500p) = 1.6 MHz
  - UGB is 30 kHz → this pole is 50× above UGB, negligible impact
  - Phase margin unchanged at ~67°

Load transient check:
  - For a 10mA load step, EA must slew gate by ~200mV
  - Slew time ≈ 200mV × 500pF / 23µA_EA ≈ 4.3µs → acceptable

This is the recommended fix: minimal component addition, no protection
gap, no startup time penalty, and negligible AC impact.

========================================================================
WHY THE SOFT-START REFERENCE DOESN'T PREVENT THIS
========================================================================

A common misconception: "The soft-start (tau=2.2ms) should prevent
inrush by ramping the reference slowly."

This is wrong for high-gain amplifiers. The EA has ~20dB DC gain (and
higher at very low frequencies due to the 1µF output cap creating an
integrator pole). Even when vref_ss is only 10mV and vfb is 0mV:

    EA output ≈ A_dc × (vref_ss − vfb) = 10 × 10mV = 100mV error signal

But in practice the EA gain at DC is much higher (two-stage OTA), and
the output saturates to the negative rail (near GND) for ANY positive
input error. The soft-start ramp cannot prevent output saturation
because the amplifier is operating in open-loop (PVDD << target, so
the loop isn't closed yet — vfb is nowhere near vref_ss).

The soft-start determines WHEN the loop closes (when vfb finally catches
up to vref_ss), not HOW MUCH current flows during the ramp. During the
entire ramp, the EA is railed and the pass device is fully ON.

To actually limit startup current, you need either:
  - A current-domain soft-start (Option D above)
  - Current limiter that doesn't oscillate (Options A, C, E)
  - No current limiter during startup (Option B)
