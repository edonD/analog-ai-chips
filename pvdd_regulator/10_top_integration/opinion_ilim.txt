OBSERVATIONS FROM PLOT
  TT 27°C  — regulates at 5.00V, starts drooping ~40mA, folds by ~55mA
  SS 150°C — regulates at 5.00V, holds flat until ~55mA, then sharp foldback
  FF -40°C — regulates at ~5.7V (0–5mA), settles to 5.0V by ~15mA, folds ~55mA

Three anomalies flagged:
  (1) FF-40 output is 5.7V at light load — violates +3.5% spec (5.175V max)
  (2) TT27 droops earlier than other corners (~40mA vs ~55mA)
  (3) Question on whether the "bandgap-referenced" comparator is correct

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(1) WHY DOES FF-40C SHOW 5.7V AT LIGHT LOAD?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Root cause: MINIMUM LOAD VIOLATION — the EA cannot turn off the pass PMOS.

At FF corner, -40°C, the pass PMOS has:
  - Lower |Vtp| (fast process → threshold reduced by ~100–200mV)
  - Higher mobility (cold temperature → µp increases ~30–40%)

Both effects mean the pass device conducts MORE current for any given Vgs.
The error amplifier drives the gate as high as it can (toward BVDD ≈ 7V) to
turn off the PMOS, but its maximum output is ~6.4V (limited by the PFET
current-source load in Stage 2, see 00_error_amp/design.cir). That leaves:

  Vgs_min = ea_out_max - BVDD = 6.4 - 7.0 = -0.6V

At FF-40, -0.6V of Vgs is enough to pass several mA through the large pass
device. With only ~10µA of load (the feedback divider), this excess current
charges the output capacitor and PVDD rises until leakage/parasitic paths
absorb the surplus — in this case to 5.7V.

As load current increases toward 15–20mA, the load sinks the excess and the
EA regains authority, pulling PVDD back to 5.0V.

This is a classic LDO minimum-load problem. The fix options are:
  (a) Add a permanent bleed resistor on PVDD: e.g., 500Ω → 10mA minimum load
      — Costs 50mW quiescent power, defeats low-Iq goal
  (b) Increase EA output swing to within ~100mV of BVDD (current-mirror load
      redesign, or add a rail-to-rail output stage)
  (c) Add a small NMOS bleeder that activates when PVDD > 5.2V (local OVP)
  (d) Accept the spec violation if the real system always draws >15mA

Severity: HIGH — the output is 14% above nominal. Downstream logic rated for
5V ±5% would see 5.7V. Could stress thin-oxide gates in 5V-tolerant I/O.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(2) WHY DOES TT27 DROP EARLIER THAN SS150 AND FF-40?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The TT27 curve begins drooping below 5.0V at ~40mA, while SS150 and FF-40
hold flat until ~55mA. This is NOT the current limiter tripping early — the
current limiter is designed for 50mA and the comparator threshold is the same
at all corners. Instead, this is an EA LOOP REGULATION issue.

Evidence: the TT27 droop is gradual (40–55mA), not a sharp fold. A comparator
trip would produce an abrupt transition like SS150 and FF-40 show.

What is happening at TT27:
  - The pass PMOS at TT27 has nominal Vtp (~-0.9V) and nominal mobility.
    To deliver 40–50mA, it needs substantial Vgs overdrive.
  - The EA output must swing low to provide this gate drive. At ~40mA, the
    EA Stage 2 NMOS (XMcs_n, W=20u L=2u) approaches its saturation limit.
  - The EA open-loop gain drops as the output transistor enters triode,
    reducing loop gain. With lower loop gain, load regulation degrades:
      ΔVout ≈ ΔIload × Rout_pass / (1 + T)
    As T (loop gain) drops, PVDD sags.

Why SS150 and FF-40 don't show this:
  - SS 150°C: PMOS |Vtp| is higher AND mobility is lower, BUT the NMOS in
    the EA also shifts. The net effect is that the EA has more margin before
    hitting its output swing limit, because the pass device needs more current
    at higher Vgs — the operating point is different.
  - FF -40°C: PMOS |Vtp| is lower, mobility is higher → the pass device needs
    LESS Vgs to deliver 50mA. The EA output stays comfortably in its linear
    range, so loop gain is maintained and regulation is flat until the current
    comparator trips sharply at ~55mA.

In short: at TT27, the EA runs out of output swing headroom before the current
comparator formally engages, creating a "soft" current limit. At FF-40, the
pass device is so efficient that the EA never stresses, so the only limiting
mechanism is the comparator (sharp fold).

Fix: Increase EA Stage 2 drive capability. Options:
  - Increase XMcs_n width (20u → 40u) for more gm and output swing
  - Increase tail current (more Iq budget)
  - Add a gain-boosting cascode in Stage 2

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
(3) IS THE BANDGAP-REFERENCED COMPARATOR WORKING CORRECTLY?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Short answer: IT WORKS, BUT THE NAME IS MISLEADING AND THE ACCURACY IS MODEST.

The current limiter comparator (04_current_limiter/design.cir) operates as:

  Isense = Iload / 1000       (sense PMOS mirror ratio)
  Iref   = ibias × 50         (NMOS mirror: W=100u/W=2u, L=8u both)
  Trip when Isense > Iref  →  Iload > ibias × 50 × 1000 = 50mA

The term "bandgap-referenced" is used because ibias is ultimately derived from
the chip's bandgap reference. However, several error sources exist:

  (a) SENSE MIRROR ACCURACY: XMs (W=1u L=0.5u) is 1/1000 of the pass device.
      But L=0.5u is near minimum length — channel-length modulation (λ) and
      Vds mismatch between the sense FET and pass device degrade the ratio.
      The cascode (XMcas) helps match Vds, but at different load currents the
      pass device Vds varies while the cascode bias is fixed from a resistor
      divider. Expect ±10–20% sense ratio error across corners.

  (b) NMOS MIRROR ACCURACY: L=8µm is good for matching (long channel), but
      the 50:1 ratio (2µm vs 100µm width) means systematic offset from edge
      effects, especially at extreme process corners. At FF-40 and SS150, the
      effective ratio could be 52:1 or 48:1.

  (c) ibias ACCURACY: If the 1µA bias has its own PVT variation (±10%), the
      trip point shifts proportionally. This is the dominant error source.

  (d) COMPARATOR SHARPNESS: The "comparator" is just the sense_out node
      voltage — when Isense exceeds Iref, sense_out rises, and an inverter
      converts this to a digital signal. There is no regenerative latch or
      hysteresis. The transition is gradual (~5–10mA of load current between
      "starting to fold" and "fully limited"), which makes the trip point
      definition ambiguous.

The plot shows trip points of ~50mA (TT27) and ~55mA (SS150, FF-40). That
is a +10% spread across PVT. For a current limiter, this is ACCEPTABLE —
most designs spec ±20–30% tolerance on current limit. The comparator is
functioning as designed.

However, the FF-40 and SS150 trip points being HIGHER than nominal (55mA vs
50mA) means the worst-case current before limiting is 55mA, not 50mA. If the
pass device SOA or bond wire fusing current is tight, this needs to be
accounted for.
