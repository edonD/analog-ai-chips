OBSERVED BEHAVIOR (from plot_load_transient.png, plot_pvt_load_transient.png):
  TT 27C:  Baseline 4.92V (80mV below 5.0V target)
           Undershoot 38mV on 1→10mA step, overshoot 43mV on 10→1mA step

==============================================================================
(1) WHY IS THE BASELINE AT 4.92V, NOT 5.0V?  (80mV DC OFFSET)
==============================================================================

The 80mV error (1.6% of target) has three contributing mechanisms:

A. FINITE DC LOOP GAIN (dominant cause, ~50-60mV contribution)

   The Bode data shows low-frequency loop gain of only ~20 dB (≈10x). This is
   extremely low for an LDO — typical designs achieve 60-80 dB DC loop gain.

   With loop gain T, the output is:
     Vout = Vtarget × T/(1+T)

   At T=10: Vout = 5.0 × 10/11 = 4.545V — a 455mV error if gain were truly
   only 10x at DC. The actual error is only 80mV, suggesting the true DC gain
   is somewhat higher than the Bode measurement indicates (the measurement may
   have been taken at a non-optimal bias point or load condition). Estimating
   from the 80mV error:

     4.92 = 5.0 × T/(1+T) → T ≈ 61.5 → ~36 dB

   This is still low. The root cause: the two-stage OTA has moderate gain.
   Stage 1 (diff pair + mirror): Av1 ≈ gm1 × (ro_n || ro_p) ≈ 25-35 dB
   Stage 2 (NFET CS + PFET load): Av2 ≈ gm_cs × (ro_n || ro_p) ≈ 15-25 dB
   But the Miller compensation Cc=20pF across Stage 2 creates a capacitive
   divider that reduces the effective DC gain at the output — the compensation
   cap provides a low-impedance path that bleeds signal current at frequencies
   above the dominant pole. The pass device gm/gds adds 10-15 dB.

   Net loop gain ≈ 35-45 dB, explaining the 80mV-class DC error.

B. ERROR AMPLIFIER SYSTEMATIC INPUT OFFSET (~10-20mV referred to output)

   The PMOS diff pair (M1/M2: W=80µm L=4µm m=2) with NMOS mirror load
   (W=20µm L=8µm m=2) has a systematic offset because:

   - The mirror load has a diode-connected side (d1) and a mirror side (d2).
     The drain voltage of d1 (diode, ~Vgs above GND) differs from d2 (set by
     Stage 2 input). This Vds mismatch across the mirror creates a systematic
     current error through channel-length modulation (lambda effect).
   - With L=8µm in the mirror, lambda is small (~0.01/V) but nonzero.
     The Vds difference × lambda × Id creates 1-2% current mismatch → a few
     mV of referred input offset → 10-20mV at the output (divided by beta).

C. FEEDBACK RESISTOR RATIO ERROR (minor, ~5-10mV contribution)

   R_TOP=364kOhm (l=536), R_BOT=118kOhm (l=174.3), both xhigh_po w=3µm.
   Beta = R_BOT/(R_TOP+R_BOT) = 118/482 = 0.2448
   Target Vout = 1.226/0.2448 = 5.008V (only 8mV above 5.0V nominal)

   The xhigh_po resistors have ~2kOhm/sq sheet resistance with process
   variation of ±15-20%. However, since both use the same material and are
   presumably laid out adjacently, the RATIO tracks well (first-order
   cancellation). Residual mismatch from random variation (Pelgrom) at
   this geometry contributes ~0.1-0.3% ratio error → 5-15mV output error.

SUMMARY: The 80mV offset is dominated by insufficient DC loop gain (~36 dB
vs. the 60+ dB needed for <10mV accuracy). The OTA needs a gain-boosting
modification or a third gain stage.

==============================================================================
(2) WHAT IS CAUSING THE RINGING?
==============================================================================

The TT 27C plot shows clear damped oscillatory ringing at both load step edges.
From the plot, the ringing period is ~50-70µs → frequency ~15-20 kHz. This is
the characteristic signature of a LIGHTLY DAMPED COMPLEX POLE PAIR in the
closed-loop transfer function — i.e., insufficient phase margin.

Root cause — inadequate pole-zero separation in the compensation:

The loop has these poles and zeros:

  p1 (dominant): Set by Cout_ext × Rout_pass
     Rout_pass ≈ 1/(gm_pass × loop_gain_before_pass) or equivalently the
     closed-loop output impedance. With 1µF Cout, this is at very low freq.

  p2 (EA internal): At the d2 node (Stage 1 output / Stage 2 input).
     Without Miller compensation: p2 ≈ gm_mirror / (Cd2_parasitic)
     With Miller: p2 is split — pushed to higher frequency by Miller effect,
     but the effective location depends on Cc, Rc, and the parasitic caps.

  p3 (output of EA / gate node): The gate node sees Cgs of the pass device
     (1mm total width → Cgs ≈ 5-15pF) plus wiring. With Rgate_path (the
     Rc=8k in the Miller network is in the signal path!), this creates:
     p3 ≈ 1/(2π × Rgate_eff × Cgate) ≈ 1/(2π × 8kΩ × 10pF) ≈ 2 MHz

  z1 (Miller Rc zero): z1 = 1/(2π × Rc × Cc) = 1/(2π × 8k × 20p) ≈ 1 MHz
     This is a LEFT-half-plane zero (Rc > 1/gm_cs_n) meant to cancel p2.

  p_load: The 200pF internal Cload creates a secondary pole with ESR of
     the output network. At ~1/(2π × Rout × 200p).

THE PROBLEM: The zero z1 at ~1 MHz is supposed to cancel p2 and provide
phase boost at the unity-gain crossover. But with UGB at only ~1-2.4 kHz
(from the Bode data), the zero is placed ~3 decades above UGB — it provides
NO phase boost where it's needed. Meanwhile, the gain rolls off through
p2 and p3 before the zero can help, and the phase accumulates to near -180°
(or worse) at the UGB, giving marginal phase margin (~30-40°, consistent
with the observed ringing and Q-factor of ~2-3).

Additionally, the 1µF external cap (Cout_ext) and 200pF internal cap (Cload)
create a pole-splitting effect at the output. The large Cout dominates, but
the 200pF in parallel creates a minor resonance with the bondwire/trace
inductance (if any).

The ringing is WORSE on the 10→1mA unload step (43mV overshoot vs 38mV
undershoot) because:
  - At light load (1mA), pass device gm drops → output impedance rises
  - Loop gain changes → effective phase margin is even worse at light load
  - The pass device enters a less linear operating region

==============================================================================
(3) WHY IS FS 150C UNSTABLE? (CONTINUOUS 200mVpp OSCILLATION)
==============================================================================

FS 150C is the worst stability corner. The oscillation is NOT transient
ringing — it is SUSTAINED, meaning the loop has NEGATIVE phase margin at
this corner. The system is genuinely unstable.

Three effects conspire at FS 150C:

A. FAST NMOS → HIGHER GAIN, WIDER BANDWIDTH

   "Fast NMOS" means lower Vth, higher mobility → higher gm for the same
   bias current. This affects:
   - Mirror load (NMOS, W=20µm L=8µm m=2): higher gm → higher Stage 1 gain
   - CS amplifier (NMOS, W=20µm L=2µm): higher gm → higher Stage 2 gain
   - The total loop gain increases by 3-6 dB at FS vs TT

   Higher loop gain means the UGB shifts to higher frequency, where there
   is LESS phase margin. If TT has ~35° PM, adding 3-6 dB of gain shifts
   UGB into the region where accumulated phase lag exceeds -180°.

B. SLOW PMOS → PASS DEVICE OUTPUT POLE SHIFTS DOWN

   "Slow PMOS" at 150C means the pass device has lower gm (higher Vth,
   lower mobility at high temp partially offset by Vth reduction — but net
   effect is reduced drive capability). This:
   - Reduces the pass device transconductance → the output pole
     p_out = gm_pass/(2π × Cout) shifts to lower frequency
   - The lower-frequency output pole adds more phase lag at the (now higher)
     UGB → phase margin erodes further

   Similarly, the PMOS tail current source and diff pair have lower gm,
   but since Stage 1 is PMOS-loaded-with-NMOS-mirror, the NMOS mirror
   dominance means Stage 1 gain INCREASES at FS.

C. 150C TEMPERATURE EFFECTS

   At 150°C:
   - All junction capacitances increase (wider depletion regions at high T
     for some junctions, but generally Cj changes are modest)
   - Subthreshold slope degrades → gm/Id ratio drops for the same operating
     point, but absolute gm changes depend on the bias current tracking
   - The bias chain (pb_tail mirrors) tracks temperature, but the NMOS/PMOS
     gain ratio shifts because they have different temperature coefficients
   - The 1µA ibias from the bandgap shifts slightly with temperature

   Net effect at 150C: the non-dominant poles shift to lower frequencies
   while the gain stays high (fast NMOS) → UGB overshoots the phase margin
   boundary.

QUANTITATIVE ESTIMATE:
   If TT 27C has PM ≈ 35° (consistent with the ringing Q ≈ 2-3), then:
   - FS adds ~3-6 dB gain → UGB doubles → phase drops ~15-20° (one octave
     of additional phase accumulation from the non-dominant poles)
   - 150C shifts poles down → additional 10-15° phase loss
   - Total: PM ≈ 35° - 25° = ~10° (barely stable) to -5° (unstable)

   At -5° to -10° PM, the loop oscillates. The 200mVpp amplitude is limited
   by slew rate of the EA and nonlinear compression of the gain stages.

==============================================================================
(4) COMPENSATION CHANGES TO FIX STABILITY
==============================================================================

The fundamental problem: the Miller compensation (Cc=20pF, Rc=8kΩ) was tuned
for typical corner with marginal phase margin (~35°). There is no margin left
for PVT variation. Target: >55° phase margin at ALL corners.

RECOMMENDED CHANGES (in priority order):

--- FIX A: INCREASE Cc TO 40-50pF (most impactful, low risk) ---

   Current: Cc = 20pF, UGB ≈ 1-2.4 kHz
   Proposed: Cc = 40pF

   Effect: UGB drops by ~2× (to ~0.5-1.2 kHz). This moves the crossover
   to a frequency with more phase margin. The bandwidth penalty is modest
   since the LDO was already slow (1-2 kHz UGB with 1µF Cout is typical).

   The transient response will be slightly slower (settling time increases
   by ~2×), but the undershoot/overshoot will be better damped because the
   higher phase margin eliminates the resonant peaking.

   Trade-off: Cc=40pF in sky130 = ~0.04mm² (MIM cap) — easily realizable.
   Going beyond 50pF has diminishing returns and costs area.

--- FIX B: REDUCE Rc TO 3-5kΩ (tune the LHP zero) ---

   Current: Rc = 8kΩ → z1 = 1/(2π × 8k × 20p) = 1 MHz (with 20pF Cc)
   With Cc=40pF: z1 = 1/(2π × 8k × 40p) = 500 kHz — still far above UGB

   Proposed: Rc = 4kΩ with Cc=40pF → z1 = 1/(2π × 4k × 40p) = 1 MHz

   Actually, the zero location with larger Cc is already better. But Rc
   also sets the RHP zero cancellation condition. The key constraint:
     Rc > 1/gm_cs_n (to keep the zero in LHP)
   With gm_cs_n ≈ 0.5-1 mA/V → 1/gm ≈ 1-2 kΩ. So Rc = 4kΩ is safe.

   More importantly: Rc is in the signal path from EA output to the gate.
   Reducing Rc from 8kΩ to 4kΩ speeds up the gate drive → improves large-
   signal transient recovery. The 8kΩ Rc with 10pF Cgs creates a 12.5 MHz
   pole, but during large-signal events the slew rate through this RC is
   limited: dV/dt = Ibias/Cc. With I_stage2 ≈ 23µA and Cc=40pF:
     Slew rate = 23µA/40pF = 0.575 V/µs
   This is adequate for the 1-2 kHz bandwidth.

--- FIX C: ADD FEEDFORWARD CAPACITOR ACROSS R_TOP (new, high impact) ---

   Add Cff across R_TOP (364kΩ) in the feedback divider:

   Cff = R_BOT × Cout / R_TOP × (desired_zero_freq_ratio)

   For a zero at ~5-10 kHz (one decade above UGB):
     Cff ≈ 1/(2π × R_TOP × fz) = 1/(2π × 364k × 10kHz) ≈ 44pF

   Proposed: Cff = 47pF across R_TOP

   This creates a zero in the feedback path:
     z_ff = 1/(2π × R_TOP × Cff) = 1/(2π × 364k × 47p) ≈ 9.3 kHz

   The feedforward zero boosts phase at frequencies above ~9 kHz, providing
   10-20° additional phase margin at the UGB. This is the classic "Type III"
   compensation technique for LDOs and is very effective.

   At high frequency, the feedback factor β approaches R_BOT/(R_BOT) = 1
   (Cff shorts R_TOP), which increases the feedback factor and pulls the
   UGB up slightly — compensated by the larger Cc.

   IMPORTANT: Cff also creates a pole at:
     p_ff = 1/(2π × (R_TOP || R_BOT) × Cff) = 1/(2π × 89k × 47p) ≈ 38 kHz
   This is well above UGB and should not cause problems.

   Trade-off: 47pF MIM cap = ~0.05mm² — easily realizable on-chip.

--- FIX D: ADD OUTPUT ESR (optional, belt-and-suspenders) ---

   Add a small series resistance with Cout_ext to create an output zero:

   Resr = 0.5-1Ω in series with 1µF Cout_ext
   z_esr = 1/(2π × Resr × Cout) = 1/(2π × 1Ω × 1µF) = 159 kHz

   This is a high-frequency zero that helps if there are additional parasitic
   poles near the UGB. For a 1-2 kHz UGB, the ESR zero at 159 kHz may not
   help much, but it provides insurance against board-level parasitics.

   Note: Real ceramic capacitors already have 10-50mΩ ESR, so this may be
   naturally present. If using a tantalum or electrolytic Cout, the ESR is
   inherently higher (0.1-1Ω) and provides this zero for free.

   Trade-off: Intentional ESR increases output ripple at switching
   frequencies (if any). For a linear LDO with no switching noise, this is
   not a concern.

--- FIX E: REDUCE STAGE 2 GAIN AT FAST CORNER (optional, harder) ---

   The Stage 2 CS amp (XMcs_n: W=20µm L=2µm) has high gm at fast corner.
   Options to tame it:
   - Increase L from 2µm to 4µm: reduces gm by ~30%, adds area
   - Add source degeneration: Rs = 1-2kΩ in XMcs_n source → gm_eff =
     gm/(1 + gm×Rs). This linearizes the gain and makes it less PVT-
     sensitive.

   This is the most robust fix for corner-to-corner variation but requires
   re-biasing the Stage 2 operating point.

==============================================================================
RECOMMENDED IMPLEMENTATION PLAN
==============================================================================

Phase 1 (immediate — fixes FS 150C oscillation):
  1. Increase Cc from 20pF to 40pF in error_amp (00_error_amp/design.cir)
  2. Reduce Rc from 8kΩ to 4kΩ (same file)
  3. Re-run AC stability (Bode) at all 4 PVT corners
  4. Target: PM > 50° at all corners, UGB > 500 Hz

Phase 2 (if Phase 1 insufficient — add feedforward):
  5. Add Cff = 47pF across R_TOP in top-level (10_top_integration/design.cir)
     Between pvdd and vfb nodes (in parallel with XFB + Cfb)
  6. Re-verify Bode at all corners

Phase 3 (if ringing persists — degenerate Stage 2):
  7. Add Rs = 1kΩ source degeneration in XMcs_n
  8. May need to adjust bias (increase pb_tail mirror ratio) to compensate
     for lost gain at SS corner

EXPECTED RESULTS AFTER PHASE 1:
  - DC baseline: still ~4.92V (DC gain unchanged by compensation)
  - Undershoot: ~45-50mV (slightly worse due to lower bandwidth)
  - Overshoot: ~35-40mV
  - Ringing: eliminated or reduced to 1-2 cycles
  - FS 150C: stable, damped transient (no oscillation)
  - Settling time: ~1-2ms (up from ~0.5ms, acceptable trade-off)

TO FIX THE 80mV DC OFFSET (separate effort):
  - Add gain: cascode the NMOS mirror load (d1/d2) to increase Stage 1
    gain by 15-20 dB. This raises DC loop gain to >50 dB → DC error < 5mV.
  - Or: trim the feedback resistor ratio to compensate (but this is a
    band-aid, not a fix — the offset will vary with temperature/process).
