DC Regulation Collapse Analysis — PVDD LDO Regulator
=====================================================
Date: 2026-04-02
Circuit: pvdd_regulator v11, SkyWater SKY130A
Symptom: PVDD holds 5.0V from 0-10mA, collapses to 2.1V at 22mA,
         then RECOVERS to 4.85V at 40mA (non-monotonic)

=====================================================================
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
=====================================================================

The collapse is caused by the current limiter falsely triggering at
~10mA due to three compounding errors, combined with a catastrophic
impedance mismatch between the limiter clamp (200um PFET) and the
error amplifier's gate drive path (23uA through 1k Rgate). The
non-monotonic recovery at 40mA is caused by a Vds-dependent shift
in the sense mirror ratio that eventually de-trips the limiter as
PVDD collapses, allowing the EA to re-assert control.

=====================================================================
(1) WHY DOES THE EA LOSE REGULATION ABOVE 10mA?
=====================================================================

The EA itself is NOT the weak link. Its two-stage OTA can swing
ea_out from ~0.5V to ~6.4V, easily covering the gate voltage range
needed for 0-50mA (gate ~5.6V at 10mA, ~4.7V at 50mA). The EA has
full gain and headroom — it is being OVERRIDDEN by the current
limiter clamp.

Three compounding errors cause the current limiter to falsely trip:

ERROR A — Sense mirror Vds mismatch (fundamental design flaw):

  The sense PMOS (XMs: W=1u L=0.5u) and the pass device (total
  W=1000u L=0.5u) share the same gate, but operate at DIFFERENT
  drain-source voltages:

    Vds_pass  = BVDD - PVDD = 7V - 5V = 2.0V  (at regulation)
    Vds_sense = BVDD - cas_mid ≈ 7V - 3.7V = 3.3V  (set by cascode)

  The cascode (XMcas) was meant to help, but it actually FIXES the
  sense Vds at ~3.3V regardless of PVDD. The pass device Vds is 2V
  at regulation. With L=0.5um (short channel), channel length
  modulation is severe:

    Isense/Ipass = (Wsense/Wpass) x (1 + lambda*Vds_sense) / (1 + lambda*Vds_pass)

  The sense device has HIGHER Vds than the pass device at regulation,
  so it conducts proportionally MORE current than the 1:1000 ratio
  predicts. The sense current OVERESTIMATES the pass current.

  For sky130 HV PMOS at L=0.5um, lambda can be 0.15-0.3 /V. At
  lambda=0.2:
    ratio_error = (1 + 0.2*3.3) / (1 + 0.2*2.0) = 1.66/1.40 = 1.186

  This is a ~19% overestimate. At 10mA load, sense = 11.9uA vs
  expected 10uA. Alone, this doesn't trip the 50uA reference.

  But there is also a narrow-width effect: XMs at W=1um vs pass
  device fingers at W=50um. In sky130, narrow HV PMOS devices can
  have |Vth| reduced by 30-80mV due to STI stress (inverse narrow
  width effect). Combined with DIBL at L=0.5um with 3.3V Vds, the
  sense device carries significantly more current than the geometric
  ratio predicts. In SPICE with proper compact models, these effects
  compound to push the effective trip point well below 50mA.

ERROR B — ibias sharing (addressed by FIX-15 in v11):

  Before v11, the error amp and current limiter shared a single 1uA
  ibias source. Both had diode-connected NMOS on the same ibias
  node, splitting the current ~0.5uA each. The limiter's reference
  mirror (50:1) then sank only 25uA instead of 50uA:

    Trip_before = 25uA x 1000 = 25mA  (halved from design intent)

  v11 fix: Dedicated Iibias_ilim (1uA) for the current limiter.
  This restores the reference to 50uA and the nominal trip to 50mA.

ERROR C — Gate pullup leakage (addressed by FIX-14 in v11):

  Before v11, XMgate_pu (W=10u L=1u) was not fully off during
  regulation because the inverter PFET (W=4u) couldn't hold
  pass_off_b cleanly at BVDD under load. XMgate_pu partially
  conducted ~1mA from BVDD into the gate node. This current had to
  be sunk by the EA through Rgate=1k, creating a ~1V offset on
  ea_out and reducing the EA's effective output range for gate drive.

  v11 fix: Inverter PFET widened to 40u (holds pass_off_b firmly at
  BVDD) and XMgate_pu weakened to W=4u L=2u (lower on-current even
  if partially activated).

COMBINED EFFECT (pre-v11):
  Error A shifts effective trip from 50mA to ~40-43mA (Vds mismatch).
  Error B halves the reference: trip from ~43mA to ~21mA.
  Error C steals 1mA from gate, reducing EA headroom, effectively
  lowering the trip to ~17mA when the EA can't overcome both the
  gate pullup current and the clamp.

THE CATASTROPHIC AMPLIFIER: Once any of these errors causes even
  PARTIAL clamp activation, the game is over. The current limiter
  clamp is 4x W=50u L=0.5u PFET (200um total) pulling gate toward
  BVDD (7V). The EA Stage 2 can only source/sink ~23uA — and it
  drives through Rgate=1k. At gate=5V with ea_out=0V, the EA
  provides 5mA through Rgate, but the 200um clamp at Vsg=2V can
  source >10mA. The clamp wins decisively. This is an impedance
  mismatch of >100:1 between attacker (clamp) and defender (EA).

  The Rgate=1k is the critical weak point. It was designed for gate
  charge isolation and slew limiting, but it creates a 1k barrier
  between the EA and the gate, while the clamp connects DIRECTLY to
  the gate node with no series resistance.

=====================================================================
(2) WHY DOES THE OUTPUT RECOVER AT 40mA?
=====================================================================

The non-monotonic recovery is the signature of a Vds-dependent sense
mirror operating in a multi-stable DC circuit. Here is the mechanism:

As the limiter clamp activates and PVDD collapses, the pass device
Vds INCREASES (Vds_pass = BVDD - PVDD):

  Load    PVDD    Vds_pass   Vds_sense   CLM ratio factor
  10mA    5.0V    2.0V       3.3V        sense OVER by ~19%
  17mA    4.3V    2.7V       3.3V        sense OVER by ~10%
  20mA    3.2V    3.8V       3.3V        sense UNDER by ~5%
  22mA    2.1V    4.9V       3.3V        sense UNDER by ~15%
  40mA    4.85V   2.15V      3.3V        sense OVER by ~17%

As PVDD drops, the pass device Vds approaches and then EXCEEDS the
sense device Vds. The CLM ratio shifts from over-sensing to
under-sensing. This means as PVDD collapses:

  - The sense current drops relative to actual load current
  - At some point, sense current falls below the 50uA reference
  - The limiter de-asserts (sense_out goes LOW, det_n goes HIGH)
  - The clamp PFETs turn OFF
  - The EA regains gate control
  - PVDD snaps back up toward 5V

At 40mA, the DC operating point is one where the EA has control
and the limiter is NOT tripped. The sense current at 40mA with
PVDD=4.85V is:
  Isense = 40mA/1000 x (1+λ*3.3)/(1+λ*2.15) ≈ 40uA x 1.17 = 46.8uA

This is just BELOW the 50uA reference — the limiter stays off.

The 10-30mA range represents a "dead zone" where the circuit
oscillates between two metastable states (EA-regulated vs
limiter-latched), and the DC sweep converges to the latched state.
Above ~35mA, only the EA-regulated state is stable.

=====================================================================
(3) IS THE CURRENT LIMITER INTERFERING IN THE 10-25mA RANGE?
=====================================================================

YES, DECISIVELY. The current limiter is the sole cause of the
regulation collapse. Without it, the EA has more than enough gain
and output range to regulate PVDD at 5.0V from 0 to 50mA+.

The interference mechanism:
  1. Sense mirror overestimates load current (Vds mismatch + narrow W)
  2. Sense current exceeds 50uA reference prematurely (~10-15mA)
  3. sense_out goes HIGH, det_n goes LOW
  4. 200um of clamp PFET activates, pulling gate toward BVDD
  5. EA fights through 1k Rgate but is overwhelmed (~23uA vs >10mA)
  6. Gate rises, pass device current drops, PVDD collapses
  7. System latches in a degraded equilibrium

The fundamental problem: the current limiter clamp has NO
proportional control. It is a bang-bang comparator with massive
output stage. Once it trips, it does not gracefully limit current —
it SLAMS the gate to BVDD through 200um of PFET. The EA has no
chance of fighting back through 1k Rgate.

=====================================================================
(4) SPECIFIC CIRCUIT CHANGES TO FIX THIS
=====================================================================

The fix requires addressing both the false trip AND the impedance
mismatch. Here are the changes in priority order:

FIX-A: MATCH THE SENSE MIRROR Vds TO THE PASS DEVICE (critical)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  The root cause is the sense PMOS operating at Vds=3.3V while the
  pass device operates at Vds=2.0V. The cascode makes this WORSE,
  not better — it pins the sense Vds at ~3.3V regardless of PVDD.

  Option 1 (recommended): Remove the cascode. Add a regulated
  cascode or replica-biased cascode that tracks PVDD:
    cas_bias = PVDD + |Vtp| + small_margin
  This forces cas_mid ≈ PVDD, so Vds_sense = BVDD - PVDD = Vds_pass.
  Perfect tracking eliminates the CLM error entirely.

  Option 2: Increase sense device length from L=0.5u to L=2u or L=4u.
  Longer L dramatically reduces CLM (lambda ∝ 1/L). Also increase
  pass device L to match, but this hurts Ron — undesirable.
  Alternatively, keep pass at L=0.5u but make sense at L=4u and
  adjust W to maintain the 1:1000 ratio at nominal operating point.
  The absolute accuracy degrades but the Vds SENSITIVITY disappears.

  Option 3: Add a replica of the pass device Vds to bias the sense
  cascode. Use a small PFET with gate=gate, source=BVDD, drain
  connected to a diode that clamps at PVDD. This creates a
  cas_bias = BVDD - Vds_pass_replica, tracking the actual operating
  point.

FIX-B: REDUCE Rgate OR ADD EA-SIDE GATE DRIVE BUFFER (critical)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Rgate=1k gives the EA only 23uA / 1k = 23mV of "authority" over
  the gate against any direct load. The clamp bypasses Rgate entirely.

  Option 1 (recommended): Reduce Rgate from 1k to 50-100 ohm.
  This gives the EA 10-20x more current into the gate node. At
  Rgate=100R, the EA can push 23uA x (ea_range/100) ≈ 50mA peak
  gate current. The clamp still has more drive, but the equilibrium
  shifts toward the EA.

  Option 2: Add a class-AB output buffer between ea_out and gate
  (emitter/source follower pair). This gives the EA low output
  impedance and high current drive directly at the gate node,
  independent of Rgate.

  Option 3: Move Rgate to AFTER the clamp connection point. Place
  a 100R between the EA buffer output and gate, with the clamp
  connected on the EA side of Rgate. This forces the clamp to fight
  the EA directly rather than bypassing the resistance.

FIX-C: ADD SERIES RESISTANCE TO THE CLAMP (moderate priority)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  The 4x 50u clamp PFETs connect directly to the gate node with
  zero series resistance. Add 1k-5k series resistance between each
  clamp PFET drain and the gate node:

    XMclamp1 gate_clamp det_n bvdd bvdd pfet ... w=50u ...
    Rclamp1 gate_clamp gate 2k

  This limits the clamp's maximum current to (BVDD - gate) / 2k
  ≈ 1mA, which the EA (with reduced Rgate) can fight. The current
  limit still functions — it just does so gracefully rather than
  slamming the gate.

FIX-D: ADD HYSTERESIS TO THE CURRENT COMPARATOR (moderate priority)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  The current limiter comparator (sense_out → det_n → clamp) has
  no hysteresis. It can oscillate at the trip boundary, causing the
  clamp to chatter. Add positive feedback to the sense_out node:

  When det_n goes LOW (clamp ON), feed back a small current (~5uA)
  into sense_out to hold it HIGH. This creates a window where the
  limiter trips at 50mA but does not release until load drops to
  ~45mA. Prevents the oscillation/latch behavior seen in the 10-30mA
  dead zone.

FIX-E: WIDEN THE SENSE DEVICE (low priority, helps accuracy)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  XMs at W=1u suffers from narrow-width Vth shift relative to the
  W=50u pass device fingers. Increase XMs to W=5u or W=10u and
  adjust the mirror ratio accordingly (reduce total pass device count
  or add sense device multiplicity). This improves Vth matching
  between sense and pass devices.

=====================================================================
RECOMMENDED MINIMUM FIX SET
=====================================================================

Apply FIX-A (Option 1: PVDD-tracking cascode) + FIX-B (Option 1:
Rgate 1k→100R) + FIX-C (2k series R on clamp drains).

These three changes:
  - Eliminate the sense mirror Vds error (root cause of false trip)
  - Give the EA 10x more gate drive authority
  - Limit the clamp's brute-force current to manageable levels
  - Preserve the current limiter's protection function at 50mA

Expected result: Clean regulation from 0-50mA, graceful current
limiting above 50mA with controlled PVDD foldback rather than
catastrophic collapse.

=====================================================================
VERIFICATION PLAN
=====================================================================

1. DC sweep: Iload 0 to 60mA, plot PVDD. Should be flat 5.0V from
   0 to ~48mA, then fold back gracefully.
2. Monitor sense_out, det_n, gate, ea_out during sweep — confirm
   limiter stays de-asserted below 48mA.
3. Transient: step load 1mA→50mA→1mA, verify no latch-up or
   oscillation during recovery.
4. PVT corners: repeat DC sweep at SS/-40C, FF/150C, FS/150C —
   sense mirror tracks correctly across corners.
5. Short circuit: Rload=0, verify clamp activates and limits Ipass
   to ~50-60mA with PVDD near 0V (protection function preserved).
