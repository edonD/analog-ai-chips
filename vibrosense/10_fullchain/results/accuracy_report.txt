--- Root Cause of Remaining Errors ---

1. NORMAL MISCLASSIFIED AS OUTER_RACE: The classifier weights expect
   normal to have the lowest envelope values, which it does. But the
   outer_race score (class 3) has large positive weights on ENV1, ENV2,
   ENV3 and large negative on ENV5. Normal has the lowest ENV5 (0.918V)
   which inflates the outer_race score. The classifier needs retraining
   on actual peak-detector feature ranges.

2. BALL MISCLASSIFIED AS INNER_RACE: Ball and inner_race have similar
   ENV4/ENV5 profiles. The classifier cannot distinguish them with
   current weights. Ball has ENV5=1.016V vs inner_race=1.040V — only
   24 mV difference, but the weight magnitudes are insufficient to
   separate them.

3. CLASSIFIER TRAINED ON ESTIMATED FEATURES: The v4 classifier was
   trained on estimated peak ranges (0.928-0.993V) which don't match
   actual simulated ranges (0.918-1.040V). Retraining on actual
   simulated features should improve accuracy.

--- What the Peak Detector Redesign Achieved ---

1. MASSIVE DYNAMIC RANGE IMPROVEMENT: Envelope spread increased from
   3-13 mV to 8-121 mV across channels. ENV4 and ENV5 now provide
   the distinctive per-channel signatures needed for classification.

2. CLASSIFIER STABILITY: The classifier no longer oscillates wildly
   between classes. Normal case is 100% stable, inner_race 84%.
   This is because peak detector outputs are slowly decaying DC values,
   not rapidly fluctuating half-wave rectifier outputs.

3. POWER REDUCTION: Peak detectors are passive (RC + behavioral diode).
   No OTA bias current needed. Analog chain power dropped from 138 uW
   to ~51 uW — a 2.7x improvement.

4. ACCURACY DOUBLED: From 1/4 (25%) to 2/4 (50%). Two fault types
   (inner_race, outer_race) are now correctly classified.

5. CLEAR PATH TO HIGHER ACCURACY: The feature spread is now sufficient.
   The remaining accuracy gap is a classifier training problem, not a
   fundamental analog limitation. Retrain classifier on actual simulated
   peak features and accuracy should improve further.
