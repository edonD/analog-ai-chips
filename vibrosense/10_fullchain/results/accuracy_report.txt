--- Root Cause Analysis ---

1. INSUFFICIENT ENVELOPE DIFFERENTIATION: All 5 envelope channels
   are within ~7 mV of VCM (0.9V) across all test cases. The maximum
   spread between any two test cases on any channel is only 6.6 mV.
   The classifier was trained on features spanning 0-1.8V, so the
   ~millivolt differences at the input are indistinguishable.

2. CLASSIFIER DEGENERACY: The classifier only ever outputs 0.45V
   (Inner Race) or 1.35V (Outer Race). It never outputs 0.0V (Normal)
   or 0.9V (Ball), indicating the weight*feature dot products for
   classes 0 and 2 are always dominated by classes 1 and 3.

3. SYNTHETIC STIMULUS LIMITATION: The bearing fault stimuli are
   synthetic multi-tone signals, not real CWRU accelerometer data.
   The spectral content may not sufficiently excite the BPF channels
   at the correct relative amplitudes to produce distinctive envelopes.

4. SIGNAL AMPLITUDE TOO LOW: With PGA gain of 4x and V_SCALE=0.02,
   the signal swing at the BPF outputs is small (~10-50 mVpp).
   The envelope detector half-wave rectifiers need larger swings
   to produce meaningful DC shifts above VCM.
