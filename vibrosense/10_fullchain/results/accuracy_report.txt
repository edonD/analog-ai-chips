--- Root Cause Analysis ---

1. ENVELOPE DYNAMIC RANGE STILL INSUFFICIENT: Despite 5x increase in
   V_SCALE (0.02->0.1), envelope spread only improved ~2x. The
   half-wave rectifier envelope detector fundamentally struggles to
   produce large DC shifts from AC signals near VCM (0.9V).

2. CLASSIFIER INSTABILITY: The retrained weights have very large
   magnitudes (up to 3069) that amplify tiny envelope differences
   into wildly swinging scores. The classifier flips between all 4
   classes depending on which envelope momentarily fluctuates.

3. TEMPORAL VARIATION: Envelopes are not static -- they fluctuate as
   the input signal varies. The classifier sees different features at
   different moments, leading to rapid class switching.

4. OUTER_RACE vs INNER_RACE CONFUSION: These two have very similar
   envelope profiles (differ by <2 mV on most channels). The
   classifier cannot reliably distinguish them.

5. SYNTHETIC STIMULUS LIMITATION: Synthetic multi-tone signals do not
   create the distinctive per-channel spectral signatures that real
   bearing fault data would provide.
