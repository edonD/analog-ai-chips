## Honest Assessment

**What works:**
- 4/4 classification accuracy on synthetic bearing fault stimuli
- Sub-100 uW power consumption (5-50x better than MCU+FFT)
- Stable classifier output (peak detector provides clean DC features)
- Open-source, fully verifiable design on SKY130

**What is unproven:**
- Generalization: 4/4 on 4 training points with 32+ free weights is not
  a rigorous accuracy metric. The classifier is almost certainly overfit.
- PVT robustness: feature shifts from process/temperature variation could
  flip thin-margin classifications (inner_race at 42%, ball at 54%).
- Real-world signals: synthetic multi-tone stimuli vs. actual CWRU data.
- Behavioral blocks: peak detector and classifier need transistor-level
  implementation for tape-out confidence.

**Bottom line:** VibroSense-1 is a successful proof-of-concept demonstrating
that sub-100 uW analog ML for vibration monitoring is architecturally sound.
The analog feature extraction chain (BPF + peak detector) produces
physically meaningful, well-separated features. The path to a real chip
requires: (1) transistor-level envelope detector, (2) PVT corner verification,
(3) validation with real accelerometer data, and (4) layout.
