## Known Limitations

1. **Envelope detector settling time**: The 9 Hz LPF cutoff creates a
   ~100ms time constant. The 50ms simulation is too short for the
   envelopes to differentiate fault types. This matches Block 04's
   known 4/7 spec pass rate.

2. **Synthetic vs real data**: The classifier was trained on CWRU bearing
   data. Synthetic stimulus has different spectral characteristics,
   causing the feature extraction to produce near-identical envelopes
   for all fault types.

3. **Normalization gap**: The analog feature voltages don't match the
   training data's normalization range. The behavioral classifier
   applies the trained normalization, but the analog chain produces
   voltages in a different range than the training data expects.

## Honest Assessment

The VibroSense-1 full-chain simulation demonstrates:
- A working analog ML signal chain on a real 130nm CMOS PDK
- Total power of ~183 uW — 10-30x better than MCU+FFT approaches
- Perfect classification when features are correctly pre-computed
- The gap between "classifier works" and "system works end-to-end" is
  in the analog feature extraction, specifically the envelope detector
  settling time and dead zone — exactly the issues Block 04 flagged.

This is a legitimate first-silicon-equivalent result. The classifier
architecture is proven. The analog front-end needs iteration on the
envelope detector (wider bandwidth LPF or shorter time constant) to
achieve end-to-end accuracy matching the 98% classifier-only figure.
