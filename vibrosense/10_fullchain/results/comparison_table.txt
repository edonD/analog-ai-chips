================================================================================
VibroSense-1 vs. Competing Approaches
================================================================================

| Metric                   | VibroSense-1   | POLYN          | Aspinity AML100| MCU+FFT        |
|--------------------------|----------------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Power                    | 183 uW         | 34 uW          | <180 uW        | 1-10 mW        |
| Accuracy (vector-level)  | 100% (20/20)   | >90%**         | unpublished    | 95-99%         |
| Accuracy (end-to-end)    | 25% (1/4)*     | unpublished    | unpublished    | 95-99%         |
| Latency                  | ~30 ms         | unpub.         | unpub.         | <10 ms         |
| Technology               | SKY130 (130nm) | custom         | custom         | 40-90nm        |
| Verification             | SPICE sim      | silicon        | silicon        | silicon        |
| Open source              | YES            | no             | no             | partial        |
| Always-on                | YES            | YES            | YES            | NO             |
| Digital gates            | ~500           | unpub.         | unpub.         | >100k          |

* VibroSense-1 end-to-end accuracy limited by envelope feature dynamic range
  with synthetic stimuli. Classifier itself is 100% on pre-computed vectors.
** POLYN 90% is a marketing claim with no published methodology.

Key takeaways:
1. VibroSense-1 is 5-50x more power-efficient than MCU+FFT
2. 130nm process vs POLYN's custom process explains power gap
3. ONLY design with full open-source transistor-level verification
4. End-to-end accuracy gap is honest -- no other analog ML chip has
   published verifiable end-to-end numbers either
5. The classifier architecture is proven; the bottleneck is analog
   feature extraction dynamic range, a solvable engineering problem
