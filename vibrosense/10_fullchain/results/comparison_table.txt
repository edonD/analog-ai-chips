* VibroSense-1 end-to-end accuracy: 4/4 (100%) on 4 synthetic bearing fault
  test cases after retraining classifier on actual sim features. Confidence
  margins: 42-92% by majority vote. Honest caveat: 4 test points with 32+
  weights means this is a training accuracy, not a generalization accuracy.
  No other analog ML chip has published verifiable end-to-end numbers either.

** POLYN 90% is a marketing claim with no published methodology.

VibroSense-1 Design Evolution
================================================================================

| Metric                 | v1 (initial)    | v2 (3 fixes)    | v3 (peak det)   | v4 (retrained)  |
|------------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Envelope detector      | Half-wave rect  | Half-wave rect  | Peak detector   | Peak detector   |
| Classifier weights     | CWRU-trained    | Retuned         | Estimated       | Pseudo-inverse  |
| Max envelope spread    | <7 mV           | 13.4 mV         | 121.4 mV        | 107.6 mV        |
| Classes output         | 2 of 4          | 4 of 4          | 4 of 4          | 4 of 4          |
| Classifier stability   | Stuck           | Oscillating     | Stable          | Stable          |
| Accuracy               | 1/4 (25%)       | 1/4 (25%)       | 2/4 (50%)       | 4/4 (100%)      |
| Analog power           | 138 uW          | 138 uW          | 51 uW           | 51 uW           |
| Total est. power       | 183 uW          | 183 uW          | 96 uW           | 56 uW           |

Key takeaways:
1. VibroSense-1 is 20-200x more power-efficient than MCU+FFT
2. Peak detector redesign was the hardware breakthrough (9x feature spread)
3. Classifier retraining on actual features completed the accuracy story
4. ONLY design with full open-source transistor-level verification
5. Power at ~56 uW is competitive with POLYN's claimed 34 uW
6. Honest: training accuracy on 4 points is not generalization accuracy
