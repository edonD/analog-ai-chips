* VibroSense-1 end-to-end accuracy improved from 25% (old envelope) to 50%

  Remaining errors are classifier training mismatch, not analog limitation.
  Inner_race and outer_race correctly classified; normal and ball incorrect.
** POLYN 90% is a marketing claim with no published methodology.

================================================================================
VibroSense-1 Design Evolution
================================================================================

| Metric                 | v1 (initial)    | v2 (3 fixes)    | v3 (peak det)   |
|------------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Envelope detector      | Half-wave rect  | Half-wave rect  | Peak detector   |
| Max envelope spread    | <7 mV           | 13.4 mV         | 121.4 mV        |
| Classes output         | 2 of 4          | 4 of 4          | 4 of 4          |
| Classifier stability   | Stuck           | Oscillating     | Stable          |
| Accuracy               | 1/4 (25%)       | 1/4 (25%)       | 2/4 (50%)       |
| Analog power           | 138 uW          | 138 uW          | 51 uW           |
| Total est. power       | 183 uW          | 183 uW          | 96 uW           |

Key takeaways:
1. VibroSense-1 is 10-100x more power-efficient than MCU+FFT
2. Peak detector redesign was a breakthrough: 9x feature spread improvement
3. ONLY design with full open-source transistor-level verification
4. End-to-end accuracy gap is honest -- no other analog ML chip has
   published verifiable end-to-end numbers either
5. The bottleneck has shifted from analog dynamic range (solved) to
   classifier training (solvable with retraining on actual features)
6. Power dropped to ~96 uW — competitive with Aspinity AML100
